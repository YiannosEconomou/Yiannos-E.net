Difficult Journeys

Double-screen video with sound, dur:7 min 18 sec, 2017

In video and cinema, as Christian Metz (*) has shown, the spectator does not identify with the characters but with the camera and the projector, the cone of light that resembles the area captured by the lens as well as the scope of eye vision. In this work there is not a single perspective set up but a multi-camera composition on two screens. Singular identification is not possible. This is not a unified and individualistic quattrocento gaze but a multiple perspective of people, of groups, of a society on the move with bumps and jolts, not in a smooth linear fashion.
