Pyrkos

Single channel video with sound, dur:6 min 33 sec, 2008

Pyrkos is a small Greek Cypriot community near the north coast of the island. They live isolated in space and time because of the particularities of the place’s recent history. Caught, as one of the inhabitants says, between the sea, the mountain and two borders with the Turkish Army - the Green Line to the East and the enclave of Kokkina to the right, they have been make a leaving for centuries by producing wood coal.


Their work, like a ritual of some ancient alchemists, carries both the secret hope for a regeneration and the futility of such belief -producing coal was never the aim of the alchemists after all. One of the solutions is to open the Green Line on that part of the world – not for ideological reasons but because of the pressing necessities of everyday life. The demand is common on both sides, because the economic pressures are also common. But, like in the rest of Cyprus, salvation is expected to come like an ex machina, from above; the state, the institutions, the UN, the EE.


However if these organisations can solve the problem it also means that they created it. And vice versa. And here in part lies the essence of the circularity of the problem in the island… Meanwhile, the inhabitants of Pyrkos go on living in a cyclical time-space scheme, where past, present and future have coalesced.

Το βίντεο «Πύρκος» παίρνει εικόνες από το γνωστό γεωγραφικά απομονωμένο χωριό στα βορειοανατολικά παράλια της Κύπρου όπου οι κάτοικοι, προσκολλημένοι στην παραγωγή κάρβουνου, μια οικονομική δραστηριότητα χωρίς μέλλον, βλέπουν τον τόπο τους να διαγράφει μια φθίνουσα πορεία. Μια διαδήλωση για να ανοίξει το οδόφραγμα του Λιμνήτη είναι κοινή με την απέναντι πλευρά, γιατί οι πιέσεις της καθημερινότητας είναι επίσης κοινές.
